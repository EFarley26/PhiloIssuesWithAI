The Cons of AI in STEM Education

Looking at the risk and harm that AI is bringing to STEM Education.

Hallucinations

One of the major issues of relying on AI for education is AI’s ability to create and then cite false information. This becomes increasingly dangerous when the user is unable to identify these hallucinations when they happen. As a result, users learn and rely upon false information hurting their education and future. AI's willingness and the frequency at which it hallucinates makes it an unreliable teacher that should not be trusted with the education of students.

Reveals the Answer

An additional issue with integrating AI in education is it poor teaching skills and habits. While AI has access to a vast array of information spread all across the internet, it has yet to learn a productive way to teach that to the user. This makes it prone to giving away the answer removing the need for students to think adn act independently. Referencing John Dewey’s educational philosophy, James M. Lang argues in his article that it is through experience and reflection that students learn best, and that tools like AI shortcut experiences, harming student learning and limiting future growth. AI is not able to give students the needed experience to help them grow and develop new skills, as it allows students to bypass challenges rather than work through them. By showing answers instead of encouraging exploration, AI skips the learning process and becomes a poor teacher that ultimately harms students’ education.

Cheating

The largest issue education has faced as a result of AI has been the rampant increase of cheating largely due to how easy AI has made it. With the click of a button, models like ChatGPT or Gemini can complete worksheets, read articles, and write essays at a high quality. Students no longer need to think to complete their homework, but can instead rely on AI for everything teachers ask of them. Additionally, as Troy Jolliemore discusses in I Used to Teach Students. Now I Catch ChatGPT Cheats this type of cheating not only harms students who fail to learn the material, but also disadvantages students who complete their work honestly. Teachers are forced to either assume AI use across the board or grade on trust alone, creating a lose-lose situation where academic integrity and fairness are undermined.

Academic Integrity

From a teaching perspective, AI limits the teacher’s ability to understand their students on a personal level. When AI completes student work or answers questions on their behalf, teachers lose insight into students’ strengths and weaknesses. This removes critical feedback needed to adjust lessons or revisit difficult topics. As James M. Lang explains, student work no longer reflects individual understanding when AI is involved, shifting the teacher’s role from educator to supervisor. Teachers are now forced to attempt to police AI use. This ultimately detracts from meaningful teaching and places instructors in an unwinnable position.
