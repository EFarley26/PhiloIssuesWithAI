The Pros of AI in STEM Education

Identifying the benefits and transofrmations AI has brought about in the STEM classroom for both students and teachers.

Accessibility

AI systems are always online and always accessible to anyone with an internet connection. This makes them a 24/7 source of knowledge with a split-second response time that simply isn’t possible by traditional teachers. Students now have a resource to ask questions and fully explore to their hearts content anytime they want to learn more. Complex topics can be explored instantly and re-explained countless times with the ability to ask continuous follow-up questions. Through AI, students’ ability to learn and develop their minds can finally keep up with their curiosity.

Personalized Learning

One of the biggest perks of embedding AI into education is AI’s ability to learn and adapt to the users’ learning style over time. Through AI, this can be done on an individual level, giving each student a dedicated resource tailored to their learning needs. At the blink of an eye, AI can switch from producing text, to generating images, to creating podcasts as new ways to teach content. As AI changes its style based on student feedback and success, it can reteach difficult concepts in new ways designed to help students thrive on a student-by-student basis. As Gattupalli argues in AI and the Philosophy of Education, AI represents a shift toward a more interactive and dialogic educational experience, where learning becomes a dynamic exchange rather than a strictly instructor-driven process. This assistance is magnified for students with disabilities, where AI can help bring out their best work.

Teaching

From a teaching perspective, AI tools offer the chance for teachers to focus more on students and their needs and less on the administrative side of the classroom. Assignment creation, editing slideshows, and even grading to a degree can be supported by AI, dramatically reducing the burden on teachers. This extra time can then be spent with students, especially those who benefit most from individual attention. In this way, AI serves as a support tool rather than a replacement for educators. This aligns with Gattupalli’s claim that as AI takes over administrative tasks, teachers are freed to focus on the social, ethical, and mentorship aspects of education where human judgment remains irreplaceable. This is particularly relevant in K–12 classrooms, where overcrowding often leaves teachers exhausted and students overlooked. Teachers can spend less time behind their desk and more time in front of real students solving real problems.
