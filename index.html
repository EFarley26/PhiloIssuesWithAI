The Goal of Education

The primary goal of the education system is to prepare students, giving them the skills necessary to succeed wherever life takes them. This is the goal regardless of the students, background, upbringing, or social status. In the United States this is supported by the federal government and the No Child Left Behind Act of 2001 which strives to see a 100% graduation rate for all students in K–12 education. This act additionally mandates state exams at certain stages of education covering mathematics and language arts topics to gauge the level of education the country is providing. Beyond typical topics, schools aim to build students’ problem-solving and critical-thinking skills, as well as provide an environment to develop social skills with their peers. This offers the perfect setting for students to gain the tools they need to propel themselves forward in life. However, recently artificial intelligence has begun to give students ways to avoid developing these skills, leaving them struggling in real-world situations.
